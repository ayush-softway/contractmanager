MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Master Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of May 1, 2026 (the "Effective Date").

BETWEEN: Nexus Digital Corp, a Delaware corporation, with its principal place of business at 200 Park Avenue, 32nd Floor, New York, NY 10166 (the "Client");

AND: the vendor identified in each Statement of Work issued hereunder (the "Vendor" or "Service Provider").

1. SCOPE OF SERVICES. Vendor shall perform the professional services described in one or more Statements of Work ("SOW") executed by the parties and incorporated herein by reference. Each SOW must be signed by an authorized representative of both parties to be binding. Client's acceptance of any deliverable constitutes acknowledgment that such deliverable conforms to the specifications set forth in the applicable SOW.

2. PAYMENT TERMS. Client shall pay undisputed invoices within sixty (60) days of receipt ("Net-60"). All payments shall be made via ACH electronic funds transfer to the bank account designated by Vendor. Client may withhold payment on any disputed line item, provided Client delivers a written dispute notice within ten (10) business days of invoice receipt specifying the basis for the dispute. Vendor shall not suspend services due to a good-faith payment dispute. Late payments on undisputed amounts shall accrue interest at 1.0% per month.

3. CONFIDENTIALITY. Each party agrees to hold the other's Confidential Information in strict confidence for a period of three (3) years from the date of disclosure. "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by one party to the other, whether orally, in writing, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure, including but not limited to business plans, customer data, financial information, product roadmaps, personnel data, and technical specifications. Vendor shall limit disclosure of Confidential Information to personnel with a need-to-know and shall require such personnel to execute confidentiality agreements at least as protective as this Agreement before receiving access.

4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY. All work product, deliverables, inventions, software, documentation, methodologies, and other materials created by Vendor specifically for Client under a SOW ("Deliverables") shall be deemed works made for hire and shall be the sole and exclusive property of Client upon full payment of all fees. To the extent any Deliverable does not qualify as a work made for hire under applicable law, Vendor hereby irrevocably assigns to Client all right, title, and interest therein, including all intellectual property rights. Vendor retains ownership of its pre-existing tools, frameworks, and background intellectual property ("Background IP") and grants Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use Background IP solely to the extent embedded in the Deliverables. Vendor shall not use Client's name, logo, or Deliverables in any marketing, case study, or portfolio without Client's prior written consent.

5. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY. NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, REGARDLESS OF THE CAUSE OF ACTION OR THE THEORY OF LIABILITY, EVEN IF SUCH PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES. VENDOR'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY TO CLIENT ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE GREATER OF (A) THE TOTAL FEES PAID TO VENDOR IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM, OR (B) FIFTY THOUSAND DOLLARS ($50,000). THE FOREGOING LIMITATIONS SHALL NOT APPLY TO BREACHES OF CONFIDENTIALITY, INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, OR GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT.

6. INDEMNIFICATION. Vendor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client and its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to: (a) Vendor's breach of this Agreement; (b) infringement of any third-party intellectual property right by the Deliverables; (c) Vendor's gross negligence or willful misconduct; or (d) any employment or labor claim brought by Vendor's personnel against Client. Client shall promptly notify Vendor of any indemnifiable claim and cooperate in the defense thereof.

7. NON-SOLICITATION. During the term of this Agreement and for eighteen (18) months following its termination or expiration, neither party shall solicit for employment or independently contract with any employee or subcontractor of the other party who was involved in performance under this Agreement, without prior written consent. A general public job posting shall not constitute solicitation for purposes of this provision.

8. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR. Vendor is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the parties. Vendor is solely responsible for all taxes, insurance, and benefits for its personnel. Vendor's personnel shall not be eligible for Client's employee benefit programs. Vendor shall maintain adequate workers' compensation, commercial general liability (minimum $2,000,000 per occurrence / $4,000,000 aggregate), and professional liability (minimum $1,000,000) insurance throughout the term and shall provide certificates of insurance upon request.

9. TERM AND TERMINATION. This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until all SOWs have been completed or terminated, unless earlier terminated. Either party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice. Client may terminate immediately upon written notice if Vendor materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within fifteen (15) days of written notice. Upon termination, Vendor shall promptly deliver all completed Deliverables and Client data, and Client shall pay for all work completed and expenses incurred through the termination date.

10. GOVERNING LAW; DISPUTE RESOLUTION. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. Any dispute arising hereunder shall be submitted first to non-binding mediation in New York, New York. If mediation fails, the parties consent to exclusive jurisdiction and venue in the state and federal courts located in New York County, New York. The prevailing party shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

11. FORCE MAJEURE. Neither party shall be in default for any delay or failure in performance resulting from causes beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, fire, flood, earthquake, pandemic, war, terrorism, government action, or internet outages. The affected party shall promptly notify the other and use commercially reasonable efforts to resume performance.

12. ENTIRE AGREEMENT. This Agreement, together with all SOWs, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, proposals, negotiations, representations, and understandings. Any purchase order, vendor registration form, or other Client procurement document issued in connection with services under this Agreement is for administrative purposes only and shall not modify the terms hereof. Amendments must be in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both parties.
